Kapampangan Cuisine

The culinary heart of the Philippines — bold flavors, rich tradition

Culinary Capital of the Philippines

A Province That Lives to Eat

Kapampangans are passionate, gifted cooks. The province's cuisine is considered the gold standard of Philippine cooking — hearty, flavorful, and deeply rooted in centuries of tradition and culture.

Must Try

Sisig

Pampanga's most famous dish — chopped pork cheeks, ears, and liver on a sizzling plate, seasoned with calamansi and chili. A Kapampangan original now loved nationwide.

Classic

Kare-Kare

A rich oxtail and vegetable stew in a thick peanut sauce, traditionally served with shrimp paste (bagoong). A festive and beloved Kapampangan staple.

Breakfast

Tocino & Longganisa

Sweet cured pork (tocino) and garlicky sausage (longganisa) are Pampanga specialties that have become breakfast staples all over the Philippines.

Dessert

Tibok-Tibok

A creamy, jiggly carabao's milk pudding unique to Pampanga. Its name comes from the heartbeat-like jiggle when you tap it.

Traditional

Burong Hipon

Fermented shrimp with cooked rice — a distinctly Kapampangan condiment and side dish with a bold, acquired flavor.

Fiesta

Morcon

A festive beef roulade stuffed with chorizo, eggs, and pickles, braised in a rich tomato sauce — reserved for special occasions.
